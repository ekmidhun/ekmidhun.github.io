Planar Guidance for Flying Through Gaps

A novel guidance method enabling quadrotors to traverse planar gaps using bearings-only information. The proposed heading direction is designed using an elliptic shaping angle derived from the angular bisector orientation of the gap-bearing angles . The stability of the resulting closed-loop kinematics is ascertained using Lyapunov’s direct method. Additionally, a phase plane analysis is carried out to visualize the safe traversal characteristics of the proposed method considering all possible initial conditions around the gap. Combined with a tracking controller, the proposed guidance strategy is applied to a six-degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) quadrotor model, ensuring convergence towards the prescribed trajectory. The effectiveness of the proposed guidance method is validated with numerical simulations and experimental flight trials.
